Margin, border and padding

Margin

"Margins in web design control the space around elements. They help create a clean look by setting the distance between elements on a webpage. By adjusting margins, designers can organize content and make it visually pleasing. Margins are like the breathing space between different parts of a webpage, making sure it looks neat and easy to read."

Border

"Borders in web design are like frames around elements. They define the edges and give a structured look to a webpage. Designers use borders to separate different sections and emphasize specific elements. Borders come in different styles, adding a creative touch to the design. By adjusting border thickness and color, designers can match them to the overall look of the webpage, guiding user attention and improving the overall experience."

Padding

"Padding in web design is the space inside an element, between its content and border. It's crucial for making content readable and not cramped. Designers adjust padding to create the right distance between text or images and the element's border.
